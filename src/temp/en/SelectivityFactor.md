# Selectivity factor

<!-- i18n:skip -->
$$\alpha = \frac{k'_{i + 1}}{k'_i}$$

- $k'_{i + 1} > k'_i$, therefore $\alpha > 1$ is required for separation to take place.

## Abbreviations

- $\alpha$

## Synonyms

- alpha
- capacity factor
- relative retention
- separation factor

## Description

The **Selectivity Factor** measures the thermodynamic separation between two peaks. It is used to determine how far apart the retention factors ($k'$) of two components are and whether a separation can be achieved.
